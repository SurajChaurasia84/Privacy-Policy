Privacy Policy for Jan Report

Last Updated: April 20, 2026

Welcome to Jan Report. We believe that transparency is the foundation of community trust. This Privacy Policy is designed to provide you with a deep and comprehensive understanding of how we handle your personal data, why we require certain permissions, and how we protect your information in our mission to improve community infrastructure.

1. Introduction and Scope

Jan Report ("we," "us," or "our") operates as a community reporting platform that connects citizens with local maintenance insights. This policy applies to all users of the Jan Report mobile application across Android and iOS platforms. By utilizing our services, you are consenting to the data practices meticulously detailed in this document.

2. Exhaustive breakdown of Data We Collect

In order to provide a functional and verifiable reporting service, we must collect specific data points. We follow the principle of data minimization, collecting only what is strictly necessary for the app's core operations.

  • A. Personal Identity via Google Authentication

    Accessing our platform requires secure authentication. When you sign in using Google Sign-In, we collect your Full Name, Email Address, and Profile Picture URL. We do this to create a verified user profile, prevent fraudulent reporting, and allow you to manage and track your own historical reports across multiple sessions. Your email address also serves as a critical communication channel for support and important policy updates.

  • B. Photographic Media (Camera and Gallery)

    The core of our service is visual verification. We require access to your Camera and storage to capture and upload images of community issues (e.g., potholes, waste, broken streetlights). These images are processed locally on your device before being securely transmitted. We do not access "private" photos from your gallery unless you explicitly select them for an issue report.

  • C. Precise Geo-Location Information (GPS)
    Why this is critical: Community reporting is meaningless without location data. We collect your Fine (Exact) and Coarse (Approximate) geographical coordinates only when you are in the process of submitting a report.

    This allows us to place your report on a geographical map so that other community members and maintenance staff can pinpoint the exact location of the reported hazard. This data is collected **only when the app is in use** and specifically during the reporting workflow.

  • D. Technical and Device Metadata

    To ensure app stability and debug technical failures, we automatically collect basic system data. This includes your Device Model, Operating System Version, unique device identifiers, and IP Address. This data is primarily used for security (preventing DDoS and spam) and performance monitoring (fixing crashes).

3. How We Use and Process Your Information

The information we collect is utilized for explicit, community-oriented purposes:

  • Verification and Validation: The photos and location timestamps provide objective evidence of reported infrastructure problems.
  • Community Awareness: Validated reports are shared on the public feed to inform others of potential hazards in their area.
  • Administrative Communication: We use your contact data to send notifications regarding the status of your reports or to request further clarification if needed.
  • Safety and Security: Detecting and preventing unauthorized access, fraudulent activities, and ensuring the platform remains safe for all users.

4. Third-Party Service Providers

We do not "sell" your data. However, we utilize high-tier technical partners to facilitate our infrastructure:

  • Cloudinary (Media Hosting): Our primary image processor. They receive and store your reported photos using high-level encryption. Cloudinary maintains its own strict privacy standards.
  • Google Cloud/Firebase: Provides our backend infrastructure and authentication mechanisms.
  • Google Maps/Places API: Used to convert your GPS coordinates into human-readable addresses.

5. Data Security and Encryption Standards

We take data security extremely seriously. All communications between your mobile device and our servers are encrypted using Transport Layer Security (TLS/SSL). We implement industry-standard administrative and technical safeguards to protect your personal information against unauthorized access, theft, or misuse.

6. Data Retention and Account Deletion

We retain your personal information for as long as necessary to provide the services described. Reports are considered public community history and may be retained indefinitely to track infrastructure trends. However, you have the Right to be Forgotten. You can request the total deletion of your profile and personal identity data at any time by contacting us at the support email provided below.

7. Children's Privacy

Jan Report is intended for users who are at least 13 years of age. We do not knowingly collect or solicit personal information from children under 13. If we become aware that we have accidentally collected personal data from a child under this age, we will delete that information immediately.

8. Changes to this Policy

We may update this Privacy Policy to reflect changes in our practices or legal obligations. We will notify you of any significant changes by posting a notification within the app or updating the "Last Updated" date at the top of this document.
